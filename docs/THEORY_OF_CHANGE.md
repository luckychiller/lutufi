# Theory of Change: Lutufi

## The Vision
Lutufi aims to enable humanity to ask and answer questions about the flow of information, risk, and influence in ways that are currently blocked by fragmented tools and methodologies.

## Input (Resources)
- Open-source development (Apache 2.0).
- Multi-disciplinary research (Math, Sociology, Economics, CS).
- Domain-specific example libraries.

## Output (What we build)
- A unified library for probabilistic inference on structural networks.
- High-performance, scalable architecture.
- Extensive documentation that focuses on "Thinking" rather than just "Code."

## Outcome (What changes for users)
1. **Researchers:** Can model uncertainty on complex networks without writing custom wrappers or hacks.
2. **Institutions:** Can perform systemic risk analysis and influence mapping with quantifiable confidence.
3. **Public Policy:** Decisions can be grounded in probabilistic forecasts of contagion (economic, health, or behavioral).

## Impact (How the world changes)
- **Reduced Fragility:** Better understanding of systemic risk reduces the probability of catastrophic failures in finance and infrastructure.
- **Improved Responses:** Faster, more accurate modeling of information spread improves public health and counter-misinformation responses.
- **Standardization:** The "Lutufi Model File" becomes a standard for shareable, reproducible computational network research.

## Assumptions
- There is a significant, unmet demand for combining Bayesian inference with social network analysis.
- Researchers will adopt a new tool if it significantly lowers their current technical friction.
- Institutions value transparency and open-source auditability over proprietary "black boxes."
