# Research Fields & Domain Examples: Lutufi

Lutufi is designed to be a "multilingual" library—not just in code, but in the scientific languages of the domains it serves. Each example in our library is treated as a short research paper: problem statement, model setup, results, and interpretation.

## 1. Epidemiology & Public Health
- **Transmission Uncertainty:** Modeling disease spread where transmission rates are not fixed but probabilistic.
- **Asymptomatic Cascades:** Using Bayesian inference to estimate the probability of "hidden" spreaders in a social contact network.
- **Behavioral Feedback:** How the *belief* of risk in a social network changes the *structure* of the contact network (e.g., social distancing).

## 2. Finance & Economics
- **Interbank Contagion:** Modeling the "too-connected-to-fail" phenomenon using interbank exposure networks.
- **Supply Chain Resilience:** Probabilistic forecasting of delays in global trade networks during systemic shocks.
- **Market Expectation Propagation:** How economic sentiment diffuses through trader networks and influences price volatility.

## 3. Political Science & Sociology
- **Opinion Dynamics:** Modeling the "echo chamber" effect as a Bayesian inference problem on a social network.
- **Coalition Stability:** Probabilistic modeling of political alliance formation and collapse.
- **Social Mobility Cascades:** How access to specific "high-influence" nodes in a social network changes an individual's economic outcome probabilities.

## 4. Intelligence & Security
- **Covert Network Reconstruction:** Using partial observations of behavior to probabilistically reconstruct the structure of hidden organizations.
- **Influence Operation Detection:** Identifying coordinated inauthentic behavior by comparing observed propagation patterns against organic probabilistic baselines.
- **Risk Propagation in Critical Infrastructure:** Modeling how a failure in the power grid probabilistically affects communication and transport networks.

## 5. Organizational Behavior
- **Information Bottlenecks:** Identifying nodes where information flow becomes highly uncertain or delayed.
- **Knowledge Loss Modeling:** Probabilistic inference of an organization's "total knowledge" if key individuals or clusters are removed.
- **Informal Hierarchy Analysis:** Mapping the "soul" of an organization (who actually influences whom) versus the "flesh" (the formal org chart).
