# License Rationale: Apache 2.0

## Choice of License
Lutufi is licensed under the **Apache License 2.0**. This choice was made with intention, balancing the needs of academic researchers, commercial entities, and institutional bodies (e.g., intelligence agencies, central banks).

## Why Not MIT?
While the MIT license is simple and permissive, it lacks explicit **patent protection**. In fields like financial modeling and network analysis, large institutional actors frequently file for patents. Apache 2.0 provides an explicit grant of patent rights from contributors to users, protecting the community from future litigation.

## Why Not GPL?
The General Public License (GPL) is "copyleft," meaning any derivative work must also be open-sourced. While ideologically noble, this aggressively closes doors for:
- **Institutional Adoption:** Many government and intelligence agencies have strict legal restrictions against using GPL-licensed software in operational environments.
- **Commercial Integration:** Companies building analytical products on top of Lutufi would be forced to open their entire software stack, which acts as a deterrent for adoption.

## Why Apache 2.0?
1. **Permissive but Protective:** It allows anyone to use, modify, and distribute Lutufi for any purpose, including commercial use, while providing patent protection.
2. **Attribution Requirement:** It requires that any redistributed version of the software includes a copy of the license and provides credit to the original authors. This ensures the name "Lutufi" travels with the work.
3. **Institutional Trust:** Apache 2.0 is the standard for major infrastructure projects (Kubernetes, TensorFlow). Adopting it signals that Lutufi is a serious, mature project ready for production use.

## Attribution Policy
When using Lutufi in published research, please cite the library and its authors as specified in the `CITATION.md` file (to be developed). This attribution is not just a legal requirement under the license, but a core part of the scientific community's social contract.
