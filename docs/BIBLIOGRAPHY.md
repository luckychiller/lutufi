# Master Bibliography: Lutufi

This document tracks the foundational papers, books, and resources that inform Lutufi's design.

## Probabilistic Graphical Models
- **Pearl, J. (1988).** *Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligent Systems: Networks of Plausible Inference.* Morgan Kaufmann. (The foundational text for Bayesian Networks).
- **Koller, D., & Friedman, N. (2009).** *Probabilistic Graphical Models: Principles and Techniques.* MIT Press. (Comprehensive reference for modern PGMs).
- **Lauritzen, S. L., & Spiegelhalter, D. J. (1988).** "Local computations with probabilities on graphical structures and their application to expert systems." *Journal of the Royal Statistical Society: Series B.*

## Network Science
- **Barabási, A. L. (2016).** *Network Science.* Cambridge University Press.
- **Watts, D. J., & Strogatz, S. H. (1998).** "Collective dynamics of 'small-world' networks." *Nature.*
- **Newman, M. E. J. (2018).** *Networks.* Oxford University Press.

## Economic & Financial Networks
- **Acemoglu, D., Ozdaglar, A., & Tahbaz-Salehi, A. (2015).** "Systemic risk and stability in financial networks." *American Economic Review.*
- **Elliott, M., Golub, B., & Jackson, M. O. (2014).** "Financial networks and cascading failures." *Econometrica.*
- **Battiston, S., et al. (2012).** "DebtRank: Too central to fail? Financial networks, the FED and systemic risk." *Scientific Reports.*

## Social Influence & Contagion
- **Granovetter, M. S. (1973).** "The strength of weak ties." *American Journal of Sociology.*
- **Kempe, D., Kleinberg, J., & Tardos, É. (2003).** "Maximizing the spread of influence through a social network." *Proceedings of the 9th ACM SIGKDD.*
- **Centola, D. (2010).** "The spread of behavior in an online social network experiment." *Science.*

## Software & Methodology
- **Hunter, J. D. (2007).** "Matplotlib: A 2D graphics environment." *Computing in Science & Engineering.*
- **Hagberg, A., Swart, P., & S Chult, D. (2008).** "Exploring network structure, dynamics, and function using NetworkX." *Los Alamos National Lab.*
- **Akanne, S., et al. (2015).** "pgmpy: A python library for learning and inference on probabilistic graphical models." *Proceedings of the 14th Python in Science Conference.*

---
*This list is dynamic and will grow as research continues during development.*
